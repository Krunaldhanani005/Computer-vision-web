Explaination

Simple business-friendly talking points for sales and demo presentations.

Face Recognition

What it does
It works like a digital receptionist, instantly recognizing registered employees and flagging strangers.
Why use it
To replace physical ID cards, speed up entry, and automate attendance without manual work.
Where it can be used
Office lobbies, factory entrances, schools, and secure facilities.
What client needs
Standard IP camera at eye level, a server or edge PC, and 3-5 photos of each employee to register them.
Expected output
An automated daily attendance sheet and real-time dashboard showing who is inside.

Restricted Area

What it does
It draws an invisible digital wall around sensitive areas and triggers an alarm if a non-approved person crosses it.
Why use it
To prevent data theft, physical sabotage, or accidents in hazardous zones automatically 24/7.
Where it can be used
Server rooms, cash counting areas, chemical labs, and CEO offices.
What client needs
CCTV camera covering the zone, a list of authorized staff photos, and network access for alerts.
Expected output
Immediate email/SMS alert with a snapshot of the intruder crossing the line.

Object Detection

What it does
It acts as a tireless set of eyes, counting specific items and spotting anomalies like misplaced boxes.
Why use it
To automate inventory checks or ensure work areas remain clean and hazard-free.
Where it can be used
Warehouses, assembly lines, retail store aisles, and loading docks.
What client needs
Standard CCTV camera and a list of specific objects they want to track (e.g., boxes, pallets, helmets).
Expected output
Live counts on a dashboard and logs of missing or unexpected items.

Vehicle Detection

What it does
It automatically reads license plates to open gates for approved cars without needing a guard.
Why use it
To eliminate traffic jams at gates, cut security guard costs, and track exact entry/exit times.
Where it can be used
Corporate parking lots, residential society gates, logistics hubs, and toll plazas.
What client needs
A specialized LPR camera aimed at the lane, decent lighting (or IR), and a database of allowed plates.
Expected output
Automatic gate opening signal and a digital log of every car that entered.
